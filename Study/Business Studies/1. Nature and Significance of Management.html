1. Nature and Significance of Management

Meaning of Management

"Management is a process of getting things done with the aim of achieving goals effectively and efficiently."

  1. Process: Series or Sequence
  2. Effectiveness: Doing right task on time
  3. Efficiency: Doing task with minimum utilisation

Features of Management

  1. It is a Goal-oriented Process.
  2. It is pervasive.
  3. It is Multi-dimensional.
    a. work: perform some task.
    b. people: human resources management.
    c. operations: activities and production cycle.
  4. It is a continous process.
  5. It is a group activity.
  6. It is a dynamic function (performed at all levels).
  7. It is an Intangible force.

Objectives of Management

  1. Organisational Objectives: It aims at prosperity and growth of the organisation.
  2. Social Objectives: It deals with the commitment of the organisation towards the society.
  3. Individual Objectives: Salary, promotion training etc.

Importance of Management (GEDPOS)

  1. It helps in achieving group goals (G)
  2. It increases efficiency. (E)
  3. It creates a dynamic function. (D)
  4. It helps in achieving personal objectives (PO)
  5. It helps in development of society (S)

Nature of Management

  1. Management as a Science: based on logically observed findings, facts and events.
    a. Systematic Body of knowledge: present in management.
    b. Principles are based on observation: partially present.
    c. Universal validity: not present.
  1. Management as an Art: requires skill, creativity and practice to get perfection
    a. Systematic Body of knowledge: present in management.
    b. Personalised application: present in management.
    c. Based on practice and creativity: present in management.
  1. Management as a Profession: entry is restricted
    a. Well defined body of knowledge: present in management.
    b. Restricted entry: soon.
    c. Presence of professional association: not compulsory.
    d. Existence of ethical codes: soon.
    e. Service motive: soon.

Levels of Management

"Levels of management” means different categories of managers, the lowest to the highest on the basis of their relative responsibilities, authority and status.

Top Level Management: planning and organising

board of directors and the chief executive or managing director.

  1. Objectives of enterprise.
  2. Framing Plans and Policies.
  3. Responsible for welfare and survival of organisation.
  4. Liaison with outside world.
  5. Integrate diverse elements and coordinate the activities.
  6. Analyse business environment and its implication.
  7. Responsible for all the activities of the business.

Middle Level Management: staffing and coordination

branch and departmental managers

  1. Interpret the policies framed by top management.
  2. Ensure that their department has necessary personnels.
  3. Assign necessary duties and responsibilities.
  4. Motivate them to achieve desired objectives.
  5. Cooperate with other departments.
  6. Responsible for all the activities of first line managers.

Lower Level Management: directing and controlling.

supervisors, foremen, section officers, superintendents

  1. Representing the problems or grievances of workers.
  2. Maintaining good working conditions and developing healthy relations between superior and subordinate.
  3. Looking to safety to workers.
  4. They try to precise standard of quality.
  5. They are responsible for boosting morale of workers.
  6. Minimise the wastage of materials and maintaining safety standards.
  7. Responsible for quality, quantity of output and loyalty of workers.

Functions of Management

Management is defined as the procedure of organising, directing, planning and controlling the efforts of organisational members and of managing organisational sources to accomplish particular goals.

  1. Planning: "deciding in advance what to do, how to do, when to do, and who's going to do."
  2. Organising: "next function, organise the activities and establish an organisation structure to execute the plan."
  3. Staffing: "recruiting, selecting, appointing the employees, assigning the duties, maintaining them duties, maintaining cordial relations and taking care of grievances of employees."
  4. Directing: "instructing the employees and get the work done."
  5. Controlling: "managers try to match the actual performance with the planned performance."

Coordination

"not a function, but it is the essence."
"a process in which manager synchronises the activities of different departments is known as coordination."

Features of Coordination

  1. Continuous Process.
  2. It is a pervasive function.
  3. It is responsibility of all managers.
  4. It is a deliberate function.

Importance of Coordination

  1. Growth in size.
  2. Functional differentiation.
  3. Specialisation.

Note: Coordination is the most important function of management.
